Discover America’s Natural Wonders

From towering mountains to vast deserts, the U.S. National Parks system offers some of the most stunning scenery in the world. Here are five must-visit parks for your next adventure.

Parks

Number Five: Great Smoky Mountains

  • Lush forests and misty mountain views
  • Most visited national park
  • Rich wildlife and biodiversity

Number Four: Yellowstone

  • First national park in the world
  • Famous for geysers like Old Faithful
  • Wildlife: bison, wolves, bears

Number Three: Zion

  • Red rock cliffs and narrow canyons
  • Popular hikes: Angels Landing, The Narrows
  • Great mix of adventure and scenery

Number Two: Grand Canyon

  • Massive canyon carved by the Colorado River
  • Incredible viewpoints and hiking trails
  • Sunrise/sunset views are a highlight

Number One: Yosemite

  • Known for granite cliffs and waterfalls
  • Landmarks: El Capitan, Half Dome
  • Great for hiking and photography

Travel Tips

  • Visit early or late in the day to avoid crowds
  • Bring plenty of water and snacks
  • Check weather conditions before your trip
  • Wear appropriate footwear for hiking
  • Respect wildlife and park rules

Bonus Section: Least Favorite National Park

Gateway Arch (Missouri)

  • Primarily an urban monument rather than a traditional nature-focused park
  • Limited outdoor exploration compared to larger national parks
  • Lacks the diverse landscapes (mountains, forests, canyons) found in other parks
  • Experience is relatively short—main attraction is the tram ride to the top
  • Less opportunity for activities like hiking, camping, or wildlife viewing
